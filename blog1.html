From Building Alone to Building Together

By Keegan Hansen-Davis • Notes from Keegan

Hey friend,

This is the first edition of Notes from Keegan. If you're reading this, you're at the absolute beginning of a new habit for both of us showing up, sharing process, and building in public.

Why this, why now

For most of my life, I tried to build things alone. I convinced myself that if I hustled hard enough in silence, eventually, success would find its way into my life. Instead, I stacked up half-started projects, a few quiet failures, and endless ideas that never quite made it out of hiding.

A little over a year ago, I found myself jobless and doubting with a new baby, a sense of total uncertainty, and the subconscious idea that I could figure it our alone conclusion, I couldn't. I learned the hard way: isolation kills momentum. We're simply not meant to build alone.

Lesson / Encouragement

What's changed for me lately? It's simple, but not easy: start before you're ready. Perfectionism and Excellence are not the same thing. All work works, It either works for you or it works on you, I've done a lot of the later. Clarity comes from action.

What I'm Building

This week I am launch Web Launch Lab my venture to give away a free custom website every single week, AND my commitment to show up publicly and bring others into the process. It's both terrifying and energizing, and after years of almost-launches, actually hitting "publish" feels like a victory in itself.

Question for You

What's one thing you've been waiting to start until it's "perfect"? What's held you back from sharing your journey or launching your idea?

Reply and let me know. I read every note, you're not building alone anymore.

Here's to doing the thing together.
